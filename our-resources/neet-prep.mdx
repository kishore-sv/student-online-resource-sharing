# NEET Preparation Guide

Stop treating NEET like a “study hard and hope” exam. It’s a **pattern-based, elimination-driven exam**. If you don’t prepare strategically, you will waste time and still score average.

This guide is focused on **what actually works**, not generic advice.

---

## 1. Understand the Exam Structure (Non-Negotiable)

- **Subjects**: Physics, Chemistry, Biology  
- **Total Questions**: 200 (180 to be attempted)  
- **Marking Scheme**: +4 (correct), -1 (wrong)  

👉 Reality:
- Biology = **rank booster (50% weightage)**
- Physics = **rank killer (most students lose marks here)**

---

## 2. Syllabus Strategy (Don’t Overcomplicate)

Stick to:
- **NCERT (Biology = Bible)**
- NCERT + basic reference for Physics & Chemistry

### Priority Order:
1. Biology (NCERT line-by-line)
2. Chemistry (NCERT + concepts)
3. Physics (concept + problem solving)

👉 Mistake people make:
> Jumping into 5 different books → zero mastery

---

## 3. Subject-Wise Strategy

### 🧬 Biology (Your Rank Decider)
- Read NCERT **at least 5–6 times**
- Focus on:
  - Diagrams
  - Tables
  - NCERT lines (questions come directly)

👉 Do:
- Make short notes from NCERT itself
- Practice MCQs daily

👉 Don’t:
- Ignore NCERT for fancy coaching material

---

### ⚗️ Chemistry (Scoring if Done Right)

Split it:

**Physical Chemistry**
- Formula + numericals
- Daily problem practice

**Organic Chemistry**
- Reaction mechanisms
- Named reactions
- Practice-based learning

**Inorganic Chemistry**
- NCERT memorization
- Revision heavy

👉 Truth:
> Inorganic = boring but highest ROI

---

### ⚡ Physics (Where Most Fail)

- Focus on:
  - Concepts → then problems
- Key areas:
  - Mechanics
  - Current Electricity
  - Modern Physics

👉 Do:
- Solve **previous year questions first**
- Then move to harder problems

👉 Don’t:
- Blindly solve tough problems without basics

---

## 4. Time Management (Be Realistic, Not Delusional)

Bad plan:
> “I’ll study 14 hours daily”

Good plan:
- 6–8 hours **consistent** study

### Sample Daily Plan:
- Biology → 2–3 hrs  
- Chemistry → 2 hrs  
- Physics → 2–3 hrs  
- Mock/Revision → 1–2 hrs  

---

## 5. Mock Tests Strategy (Game Changer)

This is where ranks are decided.

### Start Early:
- Don’t wait till syllabus completion

### After Every Test:
- Analyze:
  - Silly mistakes
  - Weak topics
  - Time wasted

👉 Rule:
> 1 test = 2–3 hours analysis

If you skip analysis → you’re wasting the test.

---

## 6. Previous Year Questions (Gold Mine)

- Solve last **10–15 years PYQs**
- Identify:
  - Repeated topics
  - Question patterns

👉 Truth:
> NEET repeats concepts, not questions

---

## 7. Revision Strategy (Most Ignored Part)

Without revision → everything collapses.

### Follow:
- Weekly revision
- Monthly full revision
- Short notes for last 30 days

👉 Use:
- Flashcards
- One-page summaries

---

## 8. Common Mistakes (Avoid These)

- Studying without solving MCQs  
- Ignoring NCERT (especially Biology & Inorganic)  
- Not analyzing mock tests  
- Overloading resources  
- Comparing with toppers instead of fixing your weak areas  

---

## 9. Final 60 Days Strategy

- Only:
  - Revision
  - Mock tests
  - Weak topic fixing  

👉 Don’t:
- Start new books  
- Panic switch strategies  

---

## 10. Mindset (This Matters More Than You Think)

- NEET is not about intelligence  
- It’s about:
  - Consistency  
  - Accuracy  
  - Smart elimination  

👉 If you’re inconsistent:
> No strategy will save you.

## Final Advice

Most students don’t fail because NEET is hard.
They fail because they:

- Study randomly
- Avoid mock tests
- Ignore revision

If you fix these three, your rank improves automatically.
